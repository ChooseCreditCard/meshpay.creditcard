What is “MeshPay” in card terms?

MeshPay is a way of thinking about payments as a network-of-networks. Instead of one fixed route (card → network → bank), the stack can choose between multiple rails, providers and flows for each transaction – optimising for cost, speed, reliability or region.

  • Multiple acquirers and processors behind the scenes
  • Card, wallet, APMs and bank transfers in one orchestration layer
  • Smart routing based on region, amount, MCC, risk and cost
  • Failover paths if one network, PSP or rail is down

The MeshPay decision layers

Checkout & UX

Which methods to show: card, wallet, BNPL, A2A, local methods – based on user, device and region.

Provider selection

Pick the best PSP/acquirer for this merchant, country, currency and risk profile.

Rail routing

Decide whether to send via card networks, instant bank rails, local schemes or wallets.

Risk & compliance

Layer fraud checks, 3DS/SCA decisions and compliance on top of routing logic.

Mesh-style routing vs single-rail setups

Aspect Single-rail Mesh-style
Networks One main card network or PSP. Multiple networks, PSPs and local methods available.
Resilience Outage = downtime. Failover path to other acquirers or rails.
Costs Limited leverage on pricing. Route to cheapest or best-performing path per segment.
Optimisation Static configuration. Dynamic rules and ML-based optimisation over time.
Coverage Good in core markets only. Fine-tuned for specific regions and local payment types.

Why MeshPay matters for merchants & users

Users just see “approved” or “declined”, but mesh-style routing can quietly improve outcomes:

  • Higher approval rates by choosing the best route for each issuer/region
  • Better UX by showing the right methods at checkout, including wallets and APMs
  • Lower processing costs by steering volume towards cheaper rails where possible
  • Less downtime thanks to automated failover between providers
  • More flexibility to plug in new rails (instant bank pay, local schemes, wallets)

MeshPay with crypto & DeFi in the mix

In more advanced stacks, crypto and DeFi act as additional value layers behind traditional rails:

  • Stablecoins and DeFi wallets funding card, wallet or APM options
  • On/off-ramp providers acting as extra nodes in the mesh
  • Token-based loyalty or cashback layered on top of card transactions
  • Dynamic choice between on-chain and off-chain rails for specific corridors
